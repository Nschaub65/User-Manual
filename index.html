Introduction and Mechanics

Page 1

Welcome to Vendetti a competitive, fast-paced card game where strategy meets speed and lots of hydration. Race against the hourglass while juggling your cards and your drink. Will you master the chaos or succumb to the pile?

Core Concept

Vendetti combines the classic card-shedding gameplay of Swoops with the pressure of a ticking timer and drinking penalties. Players race to empty both their table hand and held hand before time runs out.

What You're Trying to Accomplish

Be the first player to play all your cards from both of your hands before the hourglass runs out. If you can't play a card, you'll drink and pick up the discard pile setting you back in your quest for victory.

Gameplay Mechanics

Page 4

Rules

  1. The facedown cards MUST stay face down until they are played
  2. Players have two hands, the table hand and the held hand
    1. Players are able to play from either hand on their turn
    2. Players may also play from a combination of both hands
    3. The facedown cards in the table hand may NOT be played until the draw pile is empty
  3. If a player has two or more cards that are playable, they may play all of the duplicate cards at the same time
    1. Once a face down card has been played and subsequently revealed, the player may add any duplicate cards they may have
  4. If a player is unable to play a card, they must add the discard pile to their held hand (separate from their table hand) and take a drink
  5. Playing a 10 removes the entire discard pile from the game
  6. If all four of the same number card is played, remove the discard pile from the game
  7. After the discard pile is added to a hand or removed, the player who removed or picked up the discard pile plays the next starting card
  8. Aces are low

Playing the Game

Page 5
  1. The player to the left of the dealer places a starting card, flips the hourglass, and draws from the draw pile to refill their slot.
  2. Moving clockwise, each player plays a card less than or equal to the card currently on top of the discard pile from their available cards (hand or table).
    1. If you played a card(s) from your table hand, draw a card to fill it. If the draw pile is gone, leave the face down card uncovered.
  3. Continue clockwise until a player has no cards in either of their hands or the timer runs out

Examples of Play

Page 6

Example 1: Basic Play

The discard pile shows a 7. Player A has a 5 and a 3 in their held hand, plus a 6, 9, King, and Ace in their face-up table cards. Player A can play the 5, 3, 6, or Ace (since Ace is low). They choose to play the 5 from their hand. Play continues to the next player.

Example 2: Playing Multiples

The discard pile shows a 9. Player B has two 8s in their held hand and one 8 in their face-up table cards. Since all three are playable (8 ‚â§ 9), Player B plays all three 8s at once. They then draw one card from the draw pile to refill the empty spot in their table hand.

Example 3: Using a 10

The discard pile shows a King. Player C has no cards less than or equal to King, but they have a 10. They play the 10 (which can be played on any card). The entire discard pile is removed from the game. Player C then plays any card from their hand as the new starting card.

Example 4: Cannot Play - Must Pick Up

The discard pile shows a 4. Player D has only Queens, Kings, and a Jack remaining. None of these can be played on a 4. Player D must pick up the entire discard pile (adding all those cards to their held hand), take a drink, and then play a starting card to continue the game.

Example 5: Four of a Kind

Three 6s have already been played to the discard pile by different players. When Player A plays the fourth 6, all four are removed from the game along with the rest of the discard pile. Player A plays the next starting card.

Winning, Losing, and End-of-Game Conditions

Page 7
  • A player wins if they play all their cards from both of their hands
  • All players lose if the timer runs out before anyone can get to zero cards
  • All losing players must take a drink at the end of the game

Troubleshooting & Common Questions

Page 8

Q: What if two people play a card at the exact same time?

The player whose card physically touched the discard pile first plays their card. The other player must take their card back. If it's genuinely impossible to tell, do rock-paper-scissors to decide who played first.

Q: Can I look at my face-down table cards before the draw pile is empty?

Absolutely not! That's cheating. Face-down cards must remain a mystery until you're forced to play them after the draw pile runs out.

Q: What if the draw pile runs out mid-game?

This is normal! Once it's empty, stop drawing cards to refill table slots. Leave the face-down cards uncovered (but still face-down). You can now play those face-down cards blind - flip them as you play them.

Q: Can I play multiples from both my held hand AND table hand?

Yes! If you have three 5s (two in hand, one face-up on table) and all are playable, you can play all three at once.

Quick Reference

Page 9

Special Cards & Actions

Playing a 10
Can be played on ANY card. Clears the entire discard pile from the game. You then play the next starting card.
Four of a Kind
When the 4th card of the same rank is played, the discard pile is cleared. The player who completed it plays the next starting card.
Playing Multiples
If you have 2+ cards of the same rank that are all playable, you can play them all at once.
Cannot Play
Pick up entire discard pile + take a drink + play next starting card.
Ace Value
Aces are LOW (value of 1). Can be played on any card.

Strategy Tips

  • Manage your table hand carefully: Remember you can't play face-down cards until the draw pile is empty!
  • Save your 10s: They're powerful get-out-of-jail-free cards. Use them when you're really stuck.
  • Watch for four-of-a-kind opportunities: If three of a rank have been played, consider if you have or can get the fourth.
  • Keep track of time: Don't get too caught up strategizing. Speed matters!
  • Balance risk vs. reward: Sometimes taking the pile early (with fewer cards) is better than waiting until it's huge.
