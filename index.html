Welcome to Vendetti ‚Äî a competitive, fast-paced card game where strategy meets speed and lots of hydration. Race against the hourglass while juggling your cards and your drink. Will you master the chaos or succumb to the pile?

Core Concept

Vendetti combines the classic card-shedding gameplay of Swoops with the pressure of a ticking timer and drinking penalties. Players race to empty both their table hand and held hand before time runs out.

What You're Trying to Accomplish

Be the first player to play all your cards from both of your hands before the hourglass runs out. If you can't play a card, you'll drink and pick up the discard pile ‚Äî setting you back in your quest for victory.

Gameplay Mechanics

Page 4

Rules

  1. Face-down cards: The facedown cards MUST stay face down until they are played
  2. Two hands: Players have two hands, the table hand and the held hand
    1. Players are able to play from either hand on their turn
    2. Players may also play from a combination of both hands
    3. The facedown cards in the table hand may NOT be played until the draw pile is empty
  3. Playing multiples: If a player has two or more cards that are playable, they may play all of the duplicate cards at the same time
    1. Once a face down card has been played and subsequently revealed, the player may add any duplicate cards they may have
  4. Unable to play: If a player is unable to play a card, they must add the discard pile to their held hand (separate from their table hand) and take a drink
  5. Power of 10: Playing a 10 removes the entire discard pile from the game
  6. Four of a kind: If all four of the same number card is played, remove the discard pile from the game
  7. After pickup or removal: After the discard pile is added to a hand or removed, the player who removed or picked up the discard pile plays the next starting card
  8. Aces: Aces are low (value of 1)

Playing the Game

Page 5

Turn Structure

Each turn consists of the following steps:

  1. Starting the game: The player to the left of the dealer places a starting card from their table hand onto the discard pile, flips the hourglass, and draws from the draw pile to refill their slot.
  2. Playing a card: Moving clockwise, each player must play a card less than or equal to the card currently on top of the discard pile from their available cards (hand or table).
    1. Valid cards: You may play from your held hand OR from your face-up table cards
    2. After playing from table hand: If you played a card(s) from your table hand, draw a card from the draw pile to fill the empty spot
    3. When draw pile is empty: If the draw pile is gone, leave the face down card uncovered (but still face down)
  3. Special actions available:
    • Playing multiples: If you have 2+ cards of the same number, you can play them all at once
    • Playing a 10: You can play a 10 on ANY card. This clears the discard pile from the game
    • Completing four of a kind: If the 4th card of a number is played, the discard pile is cleared
  4. Cannot play: If you have no valid cards to play, you must:
    1. Pick up the entire discard pile and add it to your held hand
    2. Take a drink
    3. Play the next starting card
  5. Continue play: Play continues clockwise until a player has no cards in either of their hands or the timer runs out

Example 1: Basic Play

Example 2: Playing Multiples

The discard pile shows a 9. Player B has two 8s in their held hand and one 8 in their face-up table cards. Since all three are playable (8 ‚â§ 9), Player B plays all three 8s at once. They then draw one card from the draw pile to refill the empty spot in their table hand.

Example 3: Using a 10

The discard pile shows a King. Player C has no cards less than or equal to King, but they have a 10. They play the 10 (which can be played on any card). The entire discard pile is removed from the game. Player C then plays any card from their hand as the new starting card.

Example 4: Cannot Play - Must Pick Up

The discard pile shows a 4. Player D has only Queens, Kings, and a Jack remaining. None of these can be played on a 4. Player D must pick up the entire discard pile (adding all those cards to their held hand), take a drink, and then play a starting card to continue the game.

Example 5: Four of a Kind

Three 6s have already been played to the discard pile by different players. When Player A plays the fourth 6, all four are removed from the game along with the rest of the discard pile. Player A plays the next starting card.

Winning, Losing, and End-of-Game Conditions

Page 7

How the Game Ends

The game ends in one of two ways:

Victory Condition: Empty Hands

A player wins if they successfully play all their cards from both of their hands (table hand and held hand). The game ends immediately when this occurs.

Time's Up Condition

All players lose if the timer runs out before anyone can get to zero cards. The game ends immediately when the hourglass runs out.

Determining the Winner

  • Single winner: If one player empties both hands before time expires, that player wins
  • No winner: If time expires, there is no winner - all players lose together

End-of-Game Penalties

  • All losing players must take a drink at the end of the game
  • If time expires, ALL players must take a drink (even if someone was close to winning)

Tie-Breaking Procedures

Simultaneous Victory: In the extremely rare case that two players empty their hands on the same turn (for example, if Player A empties their hand, but Player B also had no cards and it's discovered after), the player who physically played their last card first is the winner.

Playing Multiple Rounds

For extended play, you can play multiple rounds and track wins:

  • First player to win 3 rounds is the ultimate champion
  • Rotate the dealer position clockwise each round
  • Players who won previous rounds may start with a slight disadvantage (optional house rule: winner starts with one extra card)
